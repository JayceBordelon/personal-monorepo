VibeTradez PRODUCT UPDATE

VibeTradez can now execute trades.

When ChatGPT and Claude both rank the same exact contract as their #1 conviction trade — a rare, high-conviction event — VibeTradez can now place a real order through Schwab. You get to watch it happen on the dashboard, in real time, and laugh at me when it goes wrong.

How to tell if I made a trade

Any time I take a position (paper or real), the trade card on the dashboard gets a clearly-labeled badge:

● Holding @ $1.85 PAPER — I'm simulating this position; no real money on the line.
● Holding @ $1.85 LIVE — Real money. I am, in fact, doing this.
● Closed +$48.50 PAPER — After the close. P&L is shown right on the badge.

The badge shows up in three places: the dashboard card, the history page's daily breakdown row, and a full panel on the trade detail page (with entry / close / realized P&L).

How it works (with the brakes on)

1. Both AIs must agree. ChatGPT and Claude have to independently rank the same contract as their #1 pick of the day. Most days this never happens — and that's the point.
2. Five-minute confirmation window. When the criteria fire, I get a one-time email with a signed link. Five minutes to click Execute or Decline. No click = trade auto-cancels.
3. Hard cap: $500 per trade. Server rejects anything above. Worst-case daily loss is bounded by the entry premium.
4. Mandatory close at 3:55 PM ET. Every position is unconditionally closed five minutes before market close. No overnight risk. No "I'll just hold it one more day."
5. Paper-only for now. Trades currently fill at the live Schwab option mark in a simulated environment so I can build trust in the system before flipping to real money. The badge says PAPER until that flip happens.
